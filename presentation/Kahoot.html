Customer Support

A journey into feedback

Created by Florentin Raud

What is Support

The customer support team is the first one to be faced with customer issues, feature requests, and problems with the product.

Support is the one who apologize to customers. Support get the deepest experience of a customer's needs.

How To Support


By adminiting there is a problem


By showing empathy


By being honnest


Think about bad previous experiences, twitter rant and robot email triggered on keyword.

Great Support


Provide solutions not excuses


Go the extra mile


Helping the customer is the goal, even if the solution is our product.

Going global

Trust is the only way you can support people 24/7

If you want to sleep, eat, spend time with your love one, you need to trust people.

Support 24H a day


It's always tea time somewhere, even if they don't do tea. Think 3 people working 9/10 hours, with 1/2/3 hour overlap. London 8 -> 18, Austin 15 -> 1, Manila 0 -> 10

Support 7 days


A primary contact / backup contact system.
At least in 2/3 of the geography involved.
Some rotation to avoid burnout.

Week end and holiday's need to be accounted for. Reduced support is better than no support.

A team effort


Using support as a way to get everyone involve in the product life.

Everyone can and should ease the customer's pain. Create a sense of ownership.

The right tool for the right job

It's not about the tool.

It's about workflow.

Simple yet powerful

Everyone in the team should be able to use it.

It should be usable by least technical people and the most technical people.

A central tool

A good tool is always used.

It should be a funnel to get all the data in. It should be a the place to go to find out about our customer.

A connected tool

A good tool has multiple usage.

It should be usable to funnel data out. It should be linked with bug tracker/feature request etc.

Policies, Procedures, Processes

What supports do needs to be written down, explicitly

Rule should be written, understood and updated. It is hard but a necessary chore.

Accountability

People needs support during their day

Tickets need to follow their owner's sun. the overlaping hours are there for handover and case collaboration.

Transparency

It is eaiser to explain when people already know

Having public statement on how we deal with SLA, bug repports, feature request help. It is easier to accept some familiar as true.

Consistency

All problems are important, some are more important than others

Defining a way to prioritise ticket is essential to provide consistant support. Every problem don't need the same level of attention.

Feedback loop

Support is the begining and the end of the feedback loop

Over time, support data is crusial to understand people.

Model the path

User needs time to understand it all

With the customer support data, we can model at what stage a user is in. Testing Kahoot, creating an account, a kahoot, presenting a kahoot. We can leverage this knowlege to help him overcome the next hurdle.

Model the experience

No one is born all knowing

With the data at hand, we can also model the level of a user of Kahoot! New comer, enthusiast, regular, expert. We can help user level up by using this knowlege.

Top 10

Until people stop interacting with us.

Using customer feedback we can focus on what are perceived as the worse pain point. This focus should also be applied on documentation.

Conclusion

With a bit of care, a global team, a central tool, a simple workflow, a few rule, some models we can help Kahoot!ers and learn something along the way.
